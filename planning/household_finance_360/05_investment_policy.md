# Investment Policy

## Purpose

This document defines how household assets should be invested based on time
horizon, not on short-term market noise.

## Goal buckets

- Emergency fund
  - Horizon: immediate
  - Risk: very low
  - Instruments: regulated savings / cash equivalents

- House expansion
  - Horizon:
  - Risk: low if under 5 years
  - Instruments: cash, term products, conservative sleeves

- Education
  - Child 1 horizon:
  - Child 2 horizon:
  - Child 3 horizon:
  - Risk: decreases as target date gets closer

- Retirement
  - Horizon: 20+ years
  - Risk: highest growth allocation in the household
  - Instruments: diversified long-term investment wrappers

## Allocation rules

- Money needed in under 3 years: no equities
- Money needed in 3 to 5 years: limited equity exposure only if clearly
  tolerated
- Money needed in 5 to 10 years: balanced allocation
- Money needed in 10+ years: growth allocation acceptable

## Rebalancing rules

- Review quarterly
- Rebalance when actual allocation drifts materially from target
- Derisk automatically as each goal approaches its target date

## Asset location rules

- Short-term reserves stay in liquid low-risk wrappers
- Long-term growth assets go into the most tax-efficient long-term wrappers
- Avoid mixing house-project money with retirement assets

## Mortgage rule

- Fixed-rate mortgage at 2.1% is not a default prepayment priority
- Extra capital should first be evaluated against:
  - liquidity needs
  - medium-term project needs
  - long-term expected investment return
